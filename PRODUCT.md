# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Teams that share a physical building and need to coordinate room usage. Office managers, team leads, and individual team members who want to quickly see availability and book a room without scheduling conflicts.

## Product Purpose

A room-booking calendar that lets co-located teams reserve shared spaces. Success looks like: a team member opens the app, sees what's available at a glance, and books a room in seconds without double-bookings or back-and-forth messages.

## Brand Personality

Precise, approachable, warm. The interface should feel calm and trustworthy. It respects people's time by being clear about availability and fast to navigate. Nothing flashy or corporate, just a reliable tool that gets out of the way.

## Anti-references

- Google Calendar: too visually dense, overwhelming information hierarchy, cluttered with features unrelated to room booking.
- Generic SaaS dashboards: metric-heavy hero layouts, gradient accents, startup energy.
- Corporate booking systems: sterile, gray, institutional feeling with tiny text and buried actions.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over density**: Show only what's needed for the next decision. Availability should be scannable in under two seconds.
2. **Respect the context**: People book rooms mid-conversation, between meetings, on the move. The interface should work fast and interrupt nothing.
3. **Quiet confidence**: The system should feel like it has everything under control. No alarms, no urgency theater, just calm certainty.
4. **Team-first**: Surfaces should orient around shared awareness. Who has what, when it's free, what's coming up.
5. **Physical grounding**: This tool manages real spaces for real people. Copy and affordances should feel tangible, not abstract.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG level mandated. Standard best practices: sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 body text minimum), keyboard navigation support, semantic markup, and reduced-motion alternatives for animations.
